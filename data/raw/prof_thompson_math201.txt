Professor: Dr. Robert Thompson
Course: MATH201 - Calculus II
Department: Mathematics
University: State University

Review 1:
Thompson is one of the most polarizing professors in the math department. Either you love his teaching style or you don't. He teaches everything from first principles and gets annoyed when students just want the formula to memorize. If you're the type who wants to understand the why behind mathematics, he's phenomenal. If you want recipes to follow, take someone else. His exams require you to prove things, not just compute.

Review 2:
Calc II with Thompson is hard. He covers all the standard topics — integration techniques, series, sequences, polar coordinates — but he goes deeper than the syllabus requires. His exams are 3 hours long and he gives you 4 problems. They're hard problems. The class average on his midterms is usually around 55-60%, which he then curves to roughly a B-. If you get above the average you're in decent shape.

Review 3:
Thompson is old school — no calculators allowed on any exam, ever. He believes if you can't do it by hand you don't understand it. He's not wrong, but it makes exam prep harder. He does offer a formula sheet for the final (one page, both sides, handwritten). His homework is from the textbook and he assigns a lot of it — expect 15-20 problems per week. He checks homework but grades it generously.

Review 4:
His lectures are brilliant but dense. He writes everything on the board and rarely uses slides. If you miss a class there's no good way to catch up except borrowing notes from someone who was there. He does not post lecture notes. He moves at a pace that assumes you're reviewing material between classes. Office hours are Tuesdays 3-5pm and by appointment. He's gruff in large lectures but surprisingly warm one-on-one.

Review 5:
Grade breakdown: homework 15%, two midterms 25% each, final 35%. He does not drop grades but the curve is applied to each exam individually. The best students in his class go on to do very well in upper-division math. Several people in my section switched sections after the first midterm — if you're going to do that, do it before the drop deadline (usually week 5). He's worth the challenge if you can handle it.
