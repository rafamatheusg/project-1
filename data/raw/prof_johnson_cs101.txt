Professor: Dr. Sarah Johnson
Course: CS101 - Introduction to Computer Science
Department: Computer Science
University: State University

Review 1:
Dr. Johnson is one of the best professors I've had at State U. Her lectures are incredibly well-organized and she always connects abstract concepts to real-world examples. CS101 with her is tough but fair. The midterm is entirely based on lecture slides — she barely touches the textbook during class. Exams are multiple choice + 2 coding problems. She curves the midterm but NOT the final, so don't slack off. Office hours are very helpful; she spends real time working through problems with you rather than just pointing at the textbook.

Review 2:
Took CS101 with Johnson spring semester. Attendance matters a lot — she does in-class exercises worth 15% of your grade and doesn't post solutions afterward. Her style is very hands-on: expect live coding during lectures. She uses Python for all examples even though the course is language-agnostic in theory. The assignments are challenging but she gives partial credit generously. Avoid taking her if you hate group projects — there's one group project worth 20% that some people loved and others hated.

Review 3:
Johnson's CS101 is legitimately one of the harder intro CS courses in the department, but she prepares you well for CS201. Her exams are hard compared to other professors teaching the same course. Grade breakdown: homework 25%, midterm 25%, group project 20%, final 30%. She does NOT drop any homework grades. If you're serious about CS, take her. If you just need the requirement, take Professor Lee instead — much easier.

Review 4:
Really enjoyed her class. She's enthusiastic and clearly loves teaching. The only complaint I have is that she moves fast — if you miss a lecture you will feel lost. Her slides are posted on Canvas but they're not as useful without her explanation. She recommends coming to class even when you're tired because she gives hints about exam topics verbally during lecture. The final covers everything from the whole semester with emphasis on the last 4 weeks.

Review 5:
Dr. Johnson gave me my first real understanding of algorithms. She explains recursion better than anyone else I've encountered. Her office hours are Tuesdays and Thursdays 2-4pm in room 312 of the CS building. She's also usually available by appointment. Very responsive on email — usually replies within 24 hours. She's strict about the syllabus deadline policy: late work loses 10% per day, no exceptions unless you contact her before the deadline.
