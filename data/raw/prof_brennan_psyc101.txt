Professor: Dr. Lisa Brennan
Course: PSYC101 - Introduction to Psychology
Department: Psychology
University: State University

Review 1:
Dr. Brennan is one of the most popular professors on campus and for good reason. Her PSYC101 lectures feel like TED talks. She's an engaging speaker who weaves in research, personal anecdotes, and demonstrations. The class is large (200+ students) but she creates a surprisingly intimate atmosphere. Exams are multiple choice + short answer. She tests on both lecture content AND textbook chapters — you need to do both.

Review 2:
Her exams are straightforward if you study both lecture slides and the assigned textbook readings. She assigns roughly 2 chapters per week from the Myers & DeWall psychology textbook. The textbook has a companion website with practice quizzes that are nearly identical in format to her exam questions — use them. Attendance is not tracked in the large lecture but she announces things verbally that aren't on slides, including what to study.

Review 3:
Brennan offers extra credit opportunities throughout the semester. Participating in psychology research studies through the department's subject pool earns up to 3% extra on your final grade. She also offers a reflection paper option for extra credit. These small boosts can matter if you're on a grade boundary. She teaches social psychology, cognitive psychology, abnormal psychology, and developmental psychology all in one semester — it's a lot of material.

Review 4:
Office hours: Tuesdays 2-4pm and Thursdays 10am-12pm in the Psych building room 412. She has a graduate student TA who also holds office hours and is excellent. Brennan is warm and accessible despite the large class size. She responds to emails but is slower during exam weeks — give her 48-72 hours. She genuinely cares whether students are learning, not just passing.

Review 5:
Grade breakdown: 3 exams (roughly evenly weighted at about 25% each) plus a final research paper (25%). The paper is a literature review on a psychology topic of your choice — she provides a list of approved topics. The paper is due the last week of class and is graded on content, argumentation, and proper APA citation format. She posts a detailed rubric. Most students do fine on the paper if they start early and visit the writing center.
